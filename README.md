# NM Radionuclide Planner

Web application for nuclear medicine radionuclide properties, decay calculations, and external dose estimation for radiation workers.

**Local use:** keep the distribution folder intact and open `index.html`, or serve the folder locally and open its `localhost` address.

Target users: medical physicists and radiation protection specialists.

---

## Features

### Properties (`index.html`)
- Database of 40 radionuclides used in nuclear medicine (PET, SPECT, therapy, brachytherapy)
  - 32 nuclides: published in Cornejo et al. (2015); operational values recalculated from ICRP 107 photon data with ICRU 57 conversion coefficients
  - 1 nuclide (Ga-68): addition beyond Cornejo, calculated from ICRP 107 photon data and ICRU 57 conversion coefficients
  - 6 therapy/imaging additions (2026-06): Zr-89, Cu-67, Tb-161, Ho-166, and the alpha-therapy chains **Ra-223+daughters** and **Ac-225+daughters** (constants include progeny in secular equilibrium; generated by `tools/add-therapy-nuclides.js`)
  - 1 nuclide (Y-90): pure β⁻ emitter with no applicable photon dose-rate constants in Properties; Dose contains a separate experimental container-dependent bremsstrahlung scenario whose constants are not tabulated by Zanzonico et al. (1999)
  - Note: Some values differ from Cornejo due to updated nuclear data in ICRP 107; material differences are shown contextually and in calculation reports
- Controlled liquid effluent **individual concentration levels** calculated from IS-28 and ICRP 119 adult ingestion coefficients. These are one condition of IS-28 Anexo II II.A.4; the mixture sum of fractions, the annual activity limits, the express discharge authorisation and the requirement that the material be soluble or readily dispersible in water must be evaluated separately (the facility's own authorisation conditions prevail).
- Filterable by category and modality

### Decay calculator (`decay.html`)
- A(t) = A₀ · e^(−λt) with interactive Chart.js v4.4.0 curve (loaded locally, no CDN dependency)
- Supports MBq, GBq, mCi, Ci
- Time to target activity

### Dose estimator (`dose.html`)
- **Dose rate at user-selected distance** (primary output) with 1 m reference column
- **H\*(10)** ambient dose equivalent (area monitoring; estimator of effective dose) and **H'(0.07)** directional dose equivalent (area monitoring; skin/extremity indicator, available for low-energy emitters) — both always shown side by side; no automatic distance-based selection. Compliance with dose limits is assessed with personal dosimetry (Hp(10), Hp(0.07), Hp(3)); the built-in limit comparison is an indicative screening only
- **Experimental Y-90 bremsstrahlung container scenario** (methodology inspired by Zanzonico et al. 1999; container values not tabulated in that publication) with interactive container selection (PMMA, Pb, W, none), explicit non-validation warning and no regulatory verdict
- Shielding: lead, elemental iron, and concrete (normal-weight and light-weight)
- **Archer broad-beam method** (Monte Carlo, Oumano et al. 2025) for Tc-99m, F-18, I-131, Lu-177
- **Spectrum-weighted narrow beam** (dose-weighted sum over ICRP 107 photon lines, NIST XCOM) for all other nuclides — accounts for beam hardening of multi-line emitters; the spectrum is stored for curated nuclides and built dynamically for ICRP 107 extended entries (`PHYSICS.buildShieldingSpectrum`); single-line narrow beam is the internal fallback when no spectrum is available
- **📄 Report / PDF** — printable calculation report with timestamp, database version, inputs, results, method, data sources and disclaimer (also in the Decay calculator)
- Cumulative dose with radioactive decay integration
- **Annual dose limit comparison** (RD 1029/2022 / EURATOM 2013/59):
  - Effective dose: 20 mSv/y worker, 1 mSv/y public
  - Extremities: 500 mSv/y
  - **Lens of eye:** Both conditions must be satisfied: 50 mSv/y max annual AND 100 mSv over any consecutive 5-year period (current regime since 22 Jun 2024)
  - Crystal lens calculation with both limits displayed
- CSV export includes dose rates at both selected distance and 1 m reference

### Extended database — ICRP 107 (`index.html`)
- Main database contains 40 curated radionuclides. For any other nuclide, the Properties page offers fallback search in **ICRP Publication 107** (1252 nuclides)
- **"Search in ICRP 107" button** appears when a nuclide is not found in the main database
- Automatically calculates Γ^H\*(10) and Γ^H'(0.07) from ICRP 107 photon emissions, including annihilation quanta where present (E ≥ 20 keV, yield ≥ 0.01%)
- Displays photon spectrum (energy, yield, type) with **max photon energy** for H'(0.07) assessment
- Constants computed from ICRP 107 are cross-checked against the published Cornejo (2015) values by the automated test suite (`node test/validate-constants.js`)
- Parser metadata: SHA256 hashes of source ICRP-107 files stored in JSON for audit trail
- See `references/icrp107/README.md` for detailed documentation on ICRP 107 integration

## Physics

### Dose rate constants
Γ [μSv·h⁻¹·GBq⁻¹·m²] = K · Σᵢ (nᵢ · h(Eᵢ))

where K = 28.648, nᵢ = photon yield per decay, h(Eᵢ) = fluence-to-dose conversion coefficient (ICRP 74).

### Shielding — Archer equation (broad beam)
T(x) = [(1 + β/α) · e^(αγx) − β/α]^(−1/γ)

Parameters from Monte Carlo simulations (GATE/MCNP6) for Tc-99m, F-18, I-131, Lu-177 in lead, normal-weight concrete and light-weight concrete.
Source: Oumano et al., *J Appl Clin Med Phys*, 2025. doi:10.1002/acm2.70084

### Shielding — spectrum-weighted narrow beam with build-up (all other nuclides)
T(x) = Σ wᵢ · min(1, B(Eᵢ, μᵢx) · e^(−μᵢx))

wᵢ = relative dose contribution of each photon line (ICRP 107 yields × ICRU 57, normalized; generated by `tools/add-shielding-spectra.js`). This captures **beam hardening** of multi-line emitters — a single-line exponential can underestimate the transmitted dose by orders of magnitude behind thick shields.

**One spectrum per operational quantity**, stored in `data/nuclides.json`: `shielding_spectrum` weights h\*(10), `shielding_spectrum_h007` weights h'(0.07). They are not interchangeable, and neither is a conservative stand-in for the other: the h'(0.07)/h\*(10) response ratio is non-monotonic (1.72 at 20 keV, 0.876 at 60 keV, 1.00 above 500 keV), so H'(0.07) weighting boosts the softest *and* the hardest lines while suppressing the 40–300 keV band. At 5 mm Pb this moves T by **−42% for Pd-103** (soft emitter) but **+8% for Re-186** (hard tail). Archer nuclides are an exception: the published fits are H\*(10) broad-beam, so their H'(0.07) row inherits the H\*(10) transmission (~1% effect for Tc-99m).

B(E, mfp) = **exposure buildup factor** for a point isotropic source, interpolated (log-B; linear in mfp, log-log in E) from the ANSI/ANS-6.4.3 standard reference data — Trubey, *NUREG/CR-5740 / ORNL/RSIC-49/R1* (1991), Table 3, Lead (with K-edge split), elemental Iron and Concrete (shared by both density variants — B depends on mean free paths, not density). The min(1, ·) clamp removes the infinite-medium artifact T > 1 at low energies. Validity 0–40 mfp and E ≥ 30 keV (Pb) / 15 keV (Fe/concrete); outside those bounds the tables clamp, which **underestimates** the transmitted dose, and the dose page warns with the fraction of the spectrum's dose weight affected. Cross-validated against the Archer Monte Carlo fits for Tc-99m/I-131: agreement within a factor ~0.5–2 at 2–10 HVL, where the pure exponential was wrong by orders of magnitude (test 12e).

μ from NIST XCOM (total attenuation, **including** coherent scattering); the lead K-edge (0.0880045 MeV, NIST) is handled explicitly and `getMu`/`getBuildup` branch on the same threshold. Single-line narrow beam (also with build-up) remains the internal fallback for any nuclide without a stored spectrum.

**Declared model limits** (not corrected — treat results as estimates, not design values): NUREG/CR-5740 derived its buildup factors *without* coherent scattering and counts mean free paths with the without-coherent coefficient, whereas μ here includes it (~4–11% on the mfp count in Pb). A larger μ lowers e^(−μx) but raises B, so **the direction of this error is not universally conservative**. The same report requires finite-barrier and tissue-interface corrections (up to 1.45 in Pb) that are not applied here, and B is an infinite-medium factor used on a finite slab.

## Data Integrity & Traceability

All generated JSON files include metadata for audit trail:

```json
{
  "notes": {
    "generated_at": "2026-06-10T19:47:50.318Z",
    "generated_by_script": "tools/parse-icrp107.js",
    "source_files_hashes": {
      "icrp107_ndx_sha256": "ac84a9cf1da890031c2ab81a33cba858ff637d701fca3bc33fb07c5a1d6cf2b9",
      "icrp107_rad_sha256": "530fde519dd062949e79439415ac7611b8b4e07ab78ce2913ffc61b5b23d4d5f",
      "icrp107_bet_sha256": "5749d7557dd22df6067c7c8d8c648ed24bf751a63ff14be42c051fe55e9dc9dd"
    },
    "parser_version": "2.0 (fixed-width columns)"
  }
}
```

- **Parser version 2.0:** FORTRAN fixed-width column extraction (not space-splitting)
- **Z values:** Derived from nuclide symbol, validated against ICRP-107
- **Hashes:** Allow verification that source files (NDX/RAD/JSON) have not been altered
- **Timestamps:** Track when data was last regenerated

## Recent Improvements & Bug Fixes (2026-06)

### Round 3 (v1.2) — buildup factors
- **Exposure build-up added to all narrow-beam shielding** (spectral and single-line): T = Σ wᵢ·min(1, B·e^(−μx)) with ANSI/ANS-6.4.3 point-isotropic exposure buildup factors (NUREG/CR-5740 Table 3; NRC/ORNL report released for unlimited distribution — the underlying ANSI/ANS-6.4.3 standard is © ANS — values transcribed programmatically and verified visually against the scan). Closes the last documented non-conservative gap — without B, transmitted dose behind thick shields was underestimated (concrete: factor 2–10+). HVL/TVL and required-thickness now solved numerically. Cross-validated against the Archer fits (ratios 0.5–2.1 at 2–10 HVL). Archer nuclides unchanged. This round added 18 tests; subsequent work has expanded the math suite further.

### Round 2 (data v1.1)
- **Spectrum-weighted narrow-beam shielding:** single-energy attenuation underestimated transmitted dose for multi-line emitters (Ga-67 behind 5 mm Pb: ×10¹² error; I-123: ×430). All non-Archer nuclides now use T(x) = Σ wᵢ·e^(−μ(Eᵢ)x) over their dose-weighted ICRP 107 lines (`shielding_spectrum`, 39 nuclides incl. the Mo-99+Tc-99m and Cs-137+Ba-137m equilibria and the Ra-223/Ac-225 chains). Archer nuclides are unaffected (their parameters already fit the full spectrum). HVL/TVL and required-thickness solve the spectral equation numerically.
- **Chain max photon energy fixed:** Ra-223 632 → 1196.3 keV, Ac-225 526.1 → 1567.1 keV (progeny lines — Pb-211 832 keV, Tl-209 1567 keV — were missed by the original generation).
- **Printable calculation reports** (`js/report.js`): Dose and Decay calculators generate a formal report (timestamp, database version, nuclide validation status, inputs, results, method, sources, disclaimer, sign-off line) via the 📄 Report / PDF button.
- **PWA:** `manifest.json`, the icons in `assets/icons/` and a cache-first service worker (`sw.js`) — installable on Android/desktop, fully offline once visited.
- **Clearance verification completed:** Lu-177 = 100 kBq/kg (plus Sm-153, Ho-166, Tm-170, Yb-175) visually confirmed against the official RD 1217/2024 PDF (p. 96).
- Decay calculator shows a secular-equilibrium note for chain entries (Ra-223, Ac-225).

### Critical
- **ICRP 107 RAD parser fix:** the block-header regex did not accept half-lives in scientific notation ("2.00E+5y"), so the photon blocks of **77 nuclides** were silently absorbed into the previous nuclide (e.g. Re-186 absorbed Re-186m, inflating its Γ by +101%; Ho-166 absorbed Ho-166m; the Ra-223/Ac-225 chain daughters had no photons at all). All 1252 RAD blocks now match their declared emission counts; Re-186 Γ^H*(10) = 3.86 matches the published Cornejo value exactly.
- **h'(0.07) column rebuilt:** the previous fluence-to-H'(0.07) column failed a kerma-ratio consistency test (implied H'(0.07)/Ka up to 3.7 — physically impossible). Rebuilt as (Ka/Φ)·hK'(0.07,0°) from ICRP 74 / PTB-Dos-34 (≤100 keV exact) plus anchors derived from Cornejo's published per-line contributions; ≥500 keV uses the kerma approximation h'(0.07)≈h*(10). Reproduces the four published Cornejo Γ^H'(0.07) values within −9.4% … +4.0% (Pd-103 −9.42%, In-111 +0.37%, I-125 +3.96%, Xe-133 +3.27%; the Pd-103 gap reflects updated ICRP-107 data). The **Dose** calculator always shows the real Γ^H'(0.07) constant in the H'(0.07) row (it exists for every photon emitter).
- **Clearance levels verified** against RD 1217/2024 Anexo IV Tabla A.1 (BOE-A-2024-25205 consolidated HTML): 28 non-null values confirmed; Ga-67 and Yb-169 are NOT listed in Tabla A.1 and now carry null.

### New
- **6 therapy/imaging nuclides added** (34 → 40): Zr-89, Cu-67, Tb-161, Ho-166, Ra-223+daughters, Ac-225+daughters. Chain constants include progeny in secular equilibrium; Ra-223+progeny agrees within 6% with the published conservative constant (~50 μSv·h⁻¹·GBq⁻¹·m²). Generated by `tools/add-therapy-nuclides.js`.
- **Ingestion e(g) and liquid effluent limits** for the 6 new nuclides taken from ICRP Publication 119 Annex F, Table F.1 (adult), via `tools/add-ingestion-coeffs.js`. The same run cross-checked the 30 pre-existing adult e(g) values against Table F.1 — all match exactly; a new test guards them.

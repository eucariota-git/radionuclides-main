Methodology — Cornejo et al. (2006)
Γ = K  ×  ∑i ( ni  ·  h(Ei) )
Γ Dose rate constant [μSv · h−1 · GBq−1 · m2] — relates dose rate to activity at 1 m
ni Photon yield per nuclear decay for emission i [dimensionless; intensity % ÷ 100]
h(Ei) Fluence-to-ambient-dose-equivalent conversion coefficient at energy Ei [pSv · cm2], tabulated in ICRU 57 / ICRP 74 for H*(10) and H′(0.07)
Ei Photon energy of emission i [MeV] — only emissions with Ei ≥ 20 keV and ni ≥ 0.01 %/decay are included (photon types G and X only)
K Unit-conversion and geometry factor = 28.648 μSv · h−1 · GBq−1 · m2 / (pSv · cm2) — encodes 1/(4π), GBq→s−1, pSv→μSv, cm−2→m−2, and s→h
